    Most music has at least three main elements - melody, rhythm and harmony.

  • Music can be a social activity, but it can also be a very spiritual experience.

  • Ancient Indians were deeply impressed by the spiritual power of music, and it is out of this that Indian classical music was born. So, for those who take it seriously, classical music involves single-minded devotion and lifelong commitment. But the thing about music is that you can take it as seriously or as casually as you like. It is a rewarding experience, no matter how deep or shallow your involvement.

  • Because of its contemplative, spiritual nature, Hindustani (north Indian) classical music is a solitary pursuit that focuses mainly on melodic development.

  • In performance, rhythm also plays an important role, giving texture, sensuality, and a sense of purpose to melody.
